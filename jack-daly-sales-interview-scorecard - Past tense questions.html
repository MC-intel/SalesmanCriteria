Candidate Information

⚠️ Interview Instructions

Focus on PAST BEHAVIOR: All questions are designed to uncover what candidates ACTUALLY DID, not what they would do. Past behavior is the best predictor of future performance. Push for specific examples with measurable results.

Listen for: Specific situations, actions taken, measurable results, and lessons learned.

Scoring Guide

5 = Exceptional - Multiple specific examples with outstanding measurable results

4 = Strong - Clear examples with good results and specifics

3 = Adequate - Basic examples but lacks detail or results

2 = Below Average - Vague answers, no specific examples

1 = Poor - Cannot provide examples or deflects questions

Follow-Up Questions to Ask Based on Their Answers:

  • For vague answers: "Can you give me specific numbers?" "What exactly did you say?" "What was the exact outcome?"
  • For success stories: "What obstacles did you face?" "Who else was involved?" "How did you measure success?"
  • For failure stories: "What would you do differently?" "What did you learn?" "How did it change your approach?"
  • Always ask: "What happened next?" "Can you give me another example?" "How typical was this situation?"

Red Flags During Interview (Beyond the Answers):

  • Cannot provide specific examples - speaks in generalities
  • Blames others for failures - no ownership
  • No measurable results to share - can't quantify success
  • Gets defensive when pressed for details
  • Stories don't add up or change when probed
  • No evidence of perseverance through adversity
  • Talks more about what they "would do" vs. what they "did do"

Green Flags to Watch For:

  • Specific examples with names, dates, and numbers
  • Takes ownership of both successes and failures
  • Shows continuous improvement from experiences
  • Demonstrates genuine care for client outcomes
  • Evidence of long-term thinking and relationships
  • Comfortable with detailed follow-up questions
  • Shows enthusiasm when discussing past challenges
